Have you and your team ever rushed to get something done in time. Maybe the team had to work overtime, and under stress. What if you didn't finish in time? Both the team and the customer might have been dissapointed.

Is there anything good about deadlines? If the team does not have ownership to the task and the product or feature, it might help them have focus. The team may be able to finish in time, and the customer might get a finished product or feature. Expecting there is a good detailed plan and the goal is clear. The customer might get exactly what they wanted, but the solution might not have been the best solution since having a deadline will force forth shortcuts and non-creativity [(1)][1].	

In my experience, having a deadline has more cons than pros. I argue that having a shared vision is better than having a deadline.


[1]: Breen, B. (2004). The 6 myths of creativity. Fast Company, 89, 75. Chicago
